Understanding dyslexia and how we can help

What is Dyslexia?

Dyslexia is a learning difference that affects reading, writing, and spelling. It is not a disease or a sign of low intelligence. People with dyslexia process written language differently, making it harder to recognize words quickly, but they often excel in creativity, problem-solving, and thinking outside the box.

Dyslexia is a natural variation in learning. Many myths still exist, like thinking dyslexic people are lazy or not smart. In reality, they just need different approaches to reading and writing, and with support, they can learn effectively and thrive.

Achievements

Many people with dyslexia have achieved great success in life. For example, Albert Einstein, Leonardo da Vinci, Agatha Christie, and Thomas Edison had dyslexia. They used their unique way of thinking to create innovations, art, and literature that changed the world. Dyslexia does not limit potential‚Äîit often brings unique strengths.

Advice for Parents and Teachers

Supporting children with dyslexia involves patience, encouragement, and understanding their unique learning style. Give them extra time to read and write, celebrate small achievements, and avoid pressure or criticism.
